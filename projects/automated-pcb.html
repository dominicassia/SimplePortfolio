Intro

This device, designed for an indoor garden project, consists of a microcontroller, relays, and external peripherals. The PCB, placed near water storage and a garden bed, uses two soil sensors to determine which area needs watering. The relays control a pump located inside the water storage and two valves correlating to the two zones monitored by the sensors. The PCB also supports a water level sensor for water storage and an external LED indicator signaling a low water level.

Overall this is a very over-complicated solution to an indoor garden, but this project was more of an experiment with PCB design.

-

Materials

1 Antique Chicken Feeder x 1
2 Bags of Soil x 2-3
3 Herb Seed Packets x 1
4 Spray Paint x 1
5 Rubber Feet x 4
6 Mini garden fencing ( optional ) x 1
-

Restoration

Rust and uneven paint layers made the surface rough.

I began hand sanding rough areas, finishing with an orbital sander. I was going for a smooth, rust-free surface. Removing all of the paint would be unnecessary. I decided to leave the inside of the feeder alone despite the rust.

I prepared for painting by washing off any remaining dust.

There happened to be a can of black gloss Rustoleum spray paint lying around, which ended up working out well. My feeder needed two coats of spray paint, which I would recommend, but paint as needed.

After drying, I hot glued four feet to the bottom of the feeder to allow for better water drainage. My feeder already had holes punched in the bottom, but I recommend punching holes to avoid drowning plants.

-
-

Planting

Since I will starting my plants as seeds, I chose seed starter soil. My feeder filled up with exactly two bags of soil, this will vary with size.

I measured four even sections within the feeder to space out the various seeds. Alternatively you could buy mini herb plants and place them within the garden. I chose chives, cilantro, oregano, and spinach for my garden.

The leafs began to shrivel up when touching the feeder, which was hot from the sun. I prevented this with some left over strawberry fencing. Tight twine or any small fencing would work just as well to prevent this.

-
-

Results

The antique chicken feeder makes an awesome mini garden for my outdoor deck. Since finishing this project, my herbs (and spinach) have grown well in the heat.

A minor detail is the drainage of the garden. The holes do not seem to drain the water properly. This is due to the flat base of the chicken feeder bed, and the inward punch of the holes. If I don't overwater the garden, it will not be a huge deal.
